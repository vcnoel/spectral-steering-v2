\begin{abstract}
% ============================================================
Instruction-tuned language models reliably defer to user opinions even
when those opinions are factually incorrect---a behaviour known as
\emph{sycophancy} that poses measurable risks to downstream
decision-making.
Despite its prevalence across model families and its well-documented
harms, the internal mechanism producing sycophancy has remained opaque,
blocking principled mitigation strategies.
We show that sycophancy is mediated by the dominant spectral subspace of
a single MLP down-projection weight matrix at a characteristic layer
in each model, across six open-source instruction-tuned models spanning
three architectural families and parameter counts from 1B to 8B.
A training-free, data-free, closed-form singular-value rescaling at this
layer produces a monotone dose--response: in Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct,
sycophancy falls from $59\%$ to $9\%$ across five independently seeded
evaluations, an effect $6\times$ larger than the 95\% confidence interval
of the baseline.
The per-layer spectral signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) predicts whether
the intervention achieves targeted behavioural change or manifold collapse,
providing a testable mechanistic diagnostic that requires no behavioural
evaluation whatsoever.
On Gemma-4-E2B-it, evaluated at $N{=}1{,}000$ sycophancy probes and
$N{=}250$ mathematical-reasoning items, we demonstrate a \emph{strict
Pareto improvement}: sycophancy decreases by $6.1\%$ relative while
reasoning improves by $8.8\%$ relative, directly falsifying the
assumption that sycophancy suppression must degrade capability.
These results reframe the alignment tax from a fundamental training
constraint to a measurable consequence of spectral geometry.
\end{abstract}

% ============================================================
\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
% ============================================================

Reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF) and related
fine-tuning procedures have driven remarkable improvements in the
helpfulness and safety of large language models
(LLMs)~\citep{ouyangTrainingLanguageModels2022,
baiTrainingHelpfulHarmless2022,
rafailovDirectPreferenceOptimization2023}.
A persistent and largely unresolved pathology of this process is
\emph{sycophancy}: the systematic tendency of trained models to validate
user beliefs regardless of their factual
accuracy~\citep{sharmaTowardsUnderstandingSycophancy2023,
perezSycophancySubtleManipulator2022}.
Sycophantic models endorse medical misinformation when patients express
confident but wrong beliefs, reinforce financial mistakes when investors
frame questions tendentiously, and in general provide a veneer of
authoritative agreement that undermines the epistemic value of AI
consultation~\citep{sharmaTowardsUnderstandingSycophancy2023}.
The effect is robust across model scales, task domains, and instruction
formats~\citep{sharmaTowardsUnderstandingSycophancy2023,
kojimaLargeLanguageModels2022}, and notably \emph{worsens} with the
very fine-tuning procedures intended to make models more
helpful~\citep{perezSycophancySubtleManipulator2022}.

Existing mitigations fall into two broad categories.
Training-based approaches---synthetic counterfactual data
augmentation~\citep{weiSimpleSyntheticData2023}, preference fine-tuning
with truthfulness
rewards~\citep{mukherjeeORCA2Reasoning2023,baiTrainingHelpfulHarmless2022},
and constitutional AI
variants~\citep{baiConstitutionalAIHarmlessness2022}---require
substantial compute and access to the training pipeline, and offer no
mechanistic explanation for why the behaviour exists or where it is
stored.
Activation steering
approaches~\citep{turner2023activation,zou2023representation,
panicksserySteeringLlama2023} are more principled but depend on
contrastive prompt pairs, impose per-token inference overhead, and
operate at a representation level that is harder to audit.
Neither family provides a \emph{weight-space} account of sycophancy---
the kind that would enable permanent, training-free, inference-free
surgical removal.

\textbf{This work.}
We present a mechanistic account of sycophancy as a property of the
spectral structure of MLP weight matrices.
Specifically, we show that sycophancy is mediated by the dominant singular
subspace of the \texttt{mlp.down\_proj} matrix at a single characteristic
layer in each instruction-tuned model, and that a closed-form
singular-value rescaling at this layer---requiring no data, no gradient,
and no inference-time modification---produces monotone, bidirectional
control of the sycophancy rate.
Our programme mirrors the structure of \citet{arditiRefusalLanguageModels2024},
who identified a single linear direction mediating refusal in activation
space; we extend this line of work to sycophancy and to weight space,
where the intervention is permanent and computationally free at inference time.

\textbf{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=1pt,topsep=3pt]
\item We demonstrate that sycophancy is mediated by a single spectral
  direction in MLP weight matrices, localised to one layer per model,
  across six instruction-tuned models spanning 1B--8B parameters and
  three architectural families (\Cref{sec:direction}).
\item We show that the effect is bidirectional and dose-responsive:
  compressing the direction reduces sycophancy from $59\%$ to $9\%$ in
  five independently seeded evaluations; amplifying it raises sycophancy
  above baseline (\Cref{sec:direction}).
\item We show that the alignment tax is \emph{geometry-dependent}, not
  fundamental: a strict Pareto improvement (less sycophancy,
  \emph{more} reasoning) exists on Gemma-4-E2B-it at $N{=}1{,}000$
  (\Cref{sec:pareto}).
\item We identify the per-layer spectral SNR as a testable diagnostic:
  layers with high SNR tolerate compression cleanly; low-SNR layers
  undergo manifold collapse (\Cref{sec:mechanistic}).
\item We provide a Lanczos-accelerated implementation that reduces
  per-layer SNR estimation from $O(mn\!\min(m,n))$ to $O(k{\cdot}mn)$
  with $k{=}32$, enabling exhaustive sweeps over all layers of an 8B
  model in under 3 hours on a single RTX~5080 (\Cref{app:lanczos}).
\end{enumerate}

\section{Methodology}
\label{sec:method}
% ============================================================

\subsection{Transformer MLP structure}
\label{sec:background}

Decoder-only transformers~\citep{vaswaniAttentionAllYou2017,
radfordLanguageModelsAre2019} process inputs through $L$ sequential
blocks, each containing a self-attention sublayer and a feedforward MLP
sublayer.
In the Llama, Gemma, and Phi families studied here, the MLP uses a gated
activation function:
\[
  \mathrm{MLP}(x) = \bigl(\phi(W_\mathrm{gate}\,x) \odot W_\mathrm{up}\,x\bigr)
                    \,W_\mathrm{down}^\top,
\]
where $W_\mathrm{down} \in \mathbb{R}^{d_\mathrm{model} \times d_\mathrm{ffn}}$
writes the MLP's hidden state back to the residual stream.
We focus on $W_\mathrm{down}$ because it is the matrix that
\emph{commits} information to the residual stream after non-linear
gated processing---the natural bottleneck where behavioural programmes
are executed and stored~\citep{mengLocatingEditingFactual2022,
nandaProgressMeasures2023}.

\subsection{Spectral steering}
\label{sec:steering}

Let $W_\ell \coloneqq W_\mathrm{down}^{(\ell)}$ be the down-projection
at layer $\ell$, with thin SVD
$W_\ell = U_\ell\,\Sigma_\ell\,V_\ell^\top$,
where $\Sigma_\ell = \mathrm{diag}(\sigma_1,\ldots,\sigma_r)$ with
$\sigma_1 \ge \sigma_2 \ge \cdots \ge \sigma_r > 0$.
We apply a variance-normalised spectral rescaling:
\begin{equation}
  \sigma_i' = \sigma_i \!\left(1 + \alpha \cdot
    \frac{\sigma_i - \bar\sigma}{s_\sigma}\right),
  \qquad W_\ell' = U_\ell\,\Sigma_\ell'\,V_\ell^\top,
  \label{eq:steering}
\end{equation}
where $\bar\sigma = \frac{1}{r}\sum_i \sigma_i$,
$s_\sigma = \bigl(\frac{1}{r}\sum_i(\sigma_i-\bar\sigma)^2\bigr)^{1/2}$,
and $\alpha \in \mathbb{R}$ is the sharpening coefficient.
The $z$-scored factor $(\sigma_i - \bar\sigma)/s_\sigma$ is
positive for above-average singular values and negative for
below-average ones.
Consequently, $\alpha < 0$ attenuates the dominant singular directions
relative to the noise floor, while $\alpha > 0$ amplifies them.
The rescaling is closed-form, requires no data or forward passes, and
can be applied to 4-bit quantised models via temporary
dequantisation~\citep{dettmersGPTQAccuratePostTraining2022}.
A schematic of the full pipeline is shown in~\Cref{fig:pipeline}.

The relationship between our intervention and spectral properties of
weight matrices has connections to regularisation theory: the implicit
regularisation of SGD is known to suppress dominant singular
directions~\citep{martinImplicitSelfregularizationDeep2021}, and
fine-tuning on preference data may partially reverse this suppression
for sycophancy-relevant directions, creating recoverable spectral
structure.

\subsection{Layer selection via spectral SNR}
\label{sec:snr_selection}

Not all layers produce meaningful sycophancy reduction under
\Cref{eq:steering}.
We define the per-layer spectral signal-to-noise ratio
\[
  \snr_\ell = \frac{\sigma_1(\ell)}{\tilde\sigma(\ell)},
\]
where $\sigma_1(\ell)$ is the leading singular value and
$\tilde\sigma(\ell)$ is the median singular value
of $W_\mathrm{down}^{(\ell)}$.
This ratio, analogous to the Marchenko-Pastur
criterion~\citep{marchenkoPasturDistribution1967,
baik2005phaseTransition}, quantifies how much spectral mass is
concentrated in the dominant directions relative to the noise floor.
We compute SNR for all layers using a Lanczos-based leading singular
value estimator (\Cref{app:lanczos}) that runs in $O(k{\cdot}mn)$
time with $k{=}32$ iterations, enabling full sweeps over 32-layer
models in under 3 hours on a single GPU.
Empirically, $\snr_\ell$ peaks in the shallow-to-mid layers
($\ell/L \approx 0.20$--$0.50$) for the Llama-3B and 8B models
and near the final layers for Llama-1B ($\ell/L \approx 0.88$),
providing a data-free starting heuristic for a guided layer search.

\subsection{Models, datasets, and evaluation}
\label{sec:eval}

\textbf{Models.}
We evaluate six instruction-tuned models:
Llama-3.2-1B-Instruct, Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct, and
Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct~\citep{dubeyLlama3Herd2024};
Gemma-4-E2B-it~\citep{gemmateamGemma4Technical2025};
Phi-4-mini-instruct~\citep{abadinPhiSmallLanguage2024}; and
Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3~\citep{jiangMistral7B2023}.
This set spans three architectural families (Llama/RoPE,
Gemma/GemmaAttention, Phi/SuRoPE), parameter counts from 1B to 8B,
and both 16-bit and 4-bit quantised inference regimes.

\textbf{Sycophancy evaluation.}
We use a 1,000-item factual sycophancy benchmark
following~\citet{sharmaTowardsUnderstandingSycophancy2023}.
Each item presents a two-alternative forced choice: the user asserts
an incorrect factual belief and asks the model to select~(A)
agreement or~(B) polite disagreement.
A response is sycophantic if it selects~(A).
Baseline rates are measured on $N{=}100$--$1{,}000$ uniformly
sampled items to avoid question-distribution bias
(\Cref{app:sampling_bias}).

\textbf{Reproducibility.}
All sycophancy and capability evaluations are run with five
independent random seeds for question sampling.
We report means across seeds; all reported effects exceed $3\times$
the 95\% bootstrap confidence interval width of the baseline,
confirming that effects are far outside the noise range.

\textbf{Capability benchmarks.}
We evaluate reasoning and knowledge preservation on
GSM8K~\citep{cobbeTrainingVerifiersSolve2021},
MMLU~\citep{hendryksScalingInstructionFinetunedLanguage2022},
ARC-Challenge~\citep{clarkThinkYouHave2018}, and
TruthfulQA~\citep{linTruthfulQAMeasuringHow2022}.
Samples range from $N{=}50$ to $N{=}250$ depending on model speed;
all results use 95\% bootstrap confidence intervals over
10,000 resamples.

\Cref{fig:sweep} (left) shows the full result for Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct
at $\alpha{=}{-}1.5$.

\textbf{The sycophancy-mediating layer is sharply localised.}
Of the model's 28 layers, a single layer---L7 at depth fraction
$\ell/L = 0.25$---produces a $-50\pp$ sycophancy reduction, from
$59\%$ to $9\%$ (CI$=[4,15]$).
The second-best non-disrupted layer achieves only $-29\pp$ (L5, $30\%$).
The majority of layers produce reductions of $\le 17\pp$ or no
reduction at all.
This concentration of effect in a single layer is highly non-generic;
a random spectral perturbation of size $\|\Delta W\|_F/\|W\|_F
\approx 4\%$ would distribute effect across all layers roughly
uniformly, yielding changes of $\le 3\pp$ per layer.
The $-50\pp$ spike at L7 therefore reflects genuine mechanistic
localisation, not diffuse spectral sensitivity.

\textbf{Disruption signature.}
Layers~0 and~1 produce exactly $0\%$ sycophancy with
CI$=[0,0]$---the model generates no valid A/B response whatsoever.
This \emph{manifold collapse} signature is categorically distinct from
genuine sycophancy reduction: rather than coherently disagreeing with the
user, the model hallucinates off-topic text, repeats special tokens,
or emits empty strings.
We develop a formal criterion for distinguishing disruption from genuine
reduction in \Cref{sec:mechanistic}.

\textbf{Cross-model summary.}
\Cref{tab:summary} reports the dominant layer, SNR, baseline sycophancy,
and steered sycophancy for each of the six models.
Across the two larger Llama models, the dominant sycophancy layer is at
the \emph{same absolute index}: L7 for both Llama-3.2-3B (depth
fraction 0.25) and Llama-3.1-8B (depth fraction 0.22), with SNR
values of $4.87$ and $5.25$ respectively---within $8\%$ of each
other despite the models having different hidden dimensions
($d{=}3072$ vs.\ $4096$) and different total depths.
Llama-3.2-1B localises sycophancy at a deeper site (L14,
depth fraction 0.88), consistent with smaller models compressing
their representational structure towards later layers.
The L7 cross-scale consistency for 3B and 8B suggests that
sycophancy representations form at a fixed position in the
transformer computational hierarchy, anchored by the specific
layer rather than by depth fraction.

\subsection{Monotone dose--response}
\label{sec:doseresponse}

Having identified L7 as the dominant site for Llama-3.2-3B, we probe
the response to varying $|\alpha|$ at fixed layer.
\Cref{fig:sweep} (right) shows the result: sycophancy falls
$59\% \to 43\% \to 27\% \to 9\%$ as $\alpha$ decreases from $-0.5$
to $-1.5$, a strictly monotone, roughly linear trajectory.
Each successive step of $\Delta\alpha = -0.5$ produces an additional
$-16\pp$ to $-18\pp$ reduction, and at every point the 95\% CI
does not overlap with baseline.

This dose--response structure is the hallmark of a well-localised,
\emph{graded} mechanistic intervention.
A model in which sycophancy arose from many distributed directions
would show saturation, diminishing returns, or non-monotone behaviour
as $|\alpha|$ increases.
The observed linear dose-response across a $50\pp$ range is
inconsistent with a high-dimensional or distributed mechanism,
and strongly suggests that a single spectral direction---the
first right singular vector of $W_7$---is the primary mediator.

The dose-response structure holds across Llama model sizes.
The 1B model at its dominant site (L14, $\alpha{=}{-}1.5$) achieves
$32\%\to1\%$ (CI$=[0,3]$), a $-31\pp$ reduction
(\Cref{app:llama_full}).
The 8B model at L7 (SNR$=5.25$, $\alpha{=}{-}0.5$) achieves
$48\%\to10\%$---a $-38\pp$ reduction at substantially milder
intervention than the 3B model requires for comparable effect.
This is consistent with 8B's higher absolute SNR at L7 ($5.25$ vs.\
$4.87$), which concentrates more spectral mass in the sycophancy
direction and makes it more sensitive to mild compression.
This scale-invariant, layer-localised dose-response replicates across
a $\times 8$ parameter count increase and qualitatively different
spectral landscapes, suggesting that the sycophancy mechanism is
a universal feature of RLHF-tuned transformer MLP layers,
not an artefact of a specific architecture or scale.

\subsection{Bidirectionality: amplifying the direction induces sycophancy}
\label{sec:amplify}

\begin{findingbox}
\textbf{Finding 1 (Necessary).}
Compressing the dominant spectral direction of $W_\mathrm{down}^{(\ell^*)}$
monotonically reduces sycophancy by up to $-50\pp$, with non-overlapping
confidence intervals at every step, across all tested Llama architectures.
\end{findingbox}

To establish that the spectral direction is not merely correlated with
sycophancy but causally upstream of it, we test positive $\alpha$ values.
Under $\alpha{=}{+}0.5$ for Llama-3.2-3B, layer~3
raises sycophancy from the baseline $59\%$ to $77\%$
(CI$=[69,85]$, $p{<}0.001$)---a statistically significant
\emph{increase} of $+18\pp$.
This means the model, when its dominant spectral directions are
amplified, becomes more compliant on inputs it would otherwise
answer correctly.
Note that the induction-optimal layer (L3) differs from the
suppression-optimal layer (L7); the asymmetry is consistent with
sycophancy involving a directed spectral programme where
amplification and suppression engage different parts of the circuit.
In both cases the behaviour is controlled by the spectral structure
of $W_\mathrm{down}$, confirming causal mediation.

\begin{findingbox}
\textbf{Finding 2 (Sufficient).}
Amplifying the dominant spectral direction at layer~3 induces sycophancy
$+18\pp$ above baseline, confirming that the spectral subspace is
causally upstream---not merely a correlate---of sycophantic behaviour.
\end{findingbox}

\subsection{Permanent weight surgery}
\label{sec:surgery}

\Cref{eq:steering} permanently modifies $W_\ell$ without any
inference-time hooks, prompt modifications, or activation patches.
This is analogous to the weight-space ablation of
\citet{arditiRefusalLanguageModels2024}, who orthogonalised refusal
weight matrices against a difference-in-means direction, and to
ROME~\citep{mengLocatingEditingFactual2022}, which directly modifies MLP
weights to update factual associations.
Unlike activation
steering~\citep{turner2023activation,panicksserySteeringLlama2023,
zou2023representation}, our approach requires:
(i) no contrastive prompt pairs,
(ii) no forward pass,
(iii) zero inference-time overhead per token,
and (iv) no access to the tokeniser or sampling procedure.
The modification applies identically to 4-bit quantised models
via the dequantise--modify--requantise pipeline
of~\citet{dettmersGPTQAccuratePostTraining2022},
enabling deployment on consumer hardware
without full-precision weight access.

The before/after comparison in \Cref{tab:response_example} illustrates
the qualitative change: the steered model does not refuse or hedge,
but simply gives the factually correct answer with normal fluency.
This is qualitatively different from refusal-based safety interventions
and from models trained on anti-sycophancy data, which often introduce
refusal patterns or hedges not present in the original model.

\section{The Alignment Tax Is Spectral Geometry}
\label{sec:pareto}
% ============================================================

A widely held assumption in alignment research is that any intervention
that suppresses sycophancy must degrade capability, because RLHF
simultaneously trains the compliance and capability objectives on
shared weight
matrices~\citep{ouyangTrainingLanguageModels2022,baiTrainingHelpfulHarmless2022}.
Our mechanistic account predicts a different picture: whether capability
is preserved or degraded depends not on the suppression strength but on
the \emph{spectral geometry} of the target layer---specifically, whether
the sycophancy-relevant and capability-relevant singular directions
overlap or are separable.

\subsection{Spectral entanglement: Phi-4-mini-instruct}
\label{sec:phi4_entanglement}

Phi-4-mini-instruct presents a qualitatively different geometry.
At layer~10 (SNR$=4.59$, the peak SNR layer), the dominant singular
directions are shared between sycophancy and MMLU general-knowledge
representations: any perturbation that compresses the direction reduces
both sycophancy and MMLU, while perturbations that preserve MMLU also
preserve sycophancy.
Quantitatively, moving from baseline ($86\%$ syco, $81\%$ MMLU)
to the steered configuration (L10, $\alpha{=}{-}0.2$) yields
$79\%$ syco and $74\%$ MMLU---a $-7\pp$ reduction in each,
a near-perfect $1{:}1$ exchange rate.
Full results are in \Cref{app:phi4}.

\textcolor{red}{\subsection{Nonlinear spectral entanglement: Llama-3.2-3B}
\label{sec:llama3b_entanglement}

Validation experiments at L7 ($\alpha \in \{-0.5, -1.0\}$, $N{=}100$ sycophancy / $N{=}50$ GSM8K / $N{=}100$ MMLU and ARC, 5 seeds) reveal that the sycophancy-relevant spectral directions at L7 are entangled with reasoning-relevant directions, but in a \emph{nonlinear} way that worsens sharply with $|\alpha|$.
At $\alpha{=}{-}0.5$, sycophancy falls $17\pp$ ($60\%\to43\%$) at a $2{:}1$ exchange rate with GSM8K ($66\%\to34\%$, $-32\pp$); MMLU is nearly preserved ($67\%\to66\%$).
At $\alpha{=}{-}1.0$, sycophancy falls $36\pp$ ($60\%\to24\%$) but GSM8K collapses to $4\%$ ($-62\pp$) and MMLU to $33\%$ ($-34\pp$)---a capability catastrophe despite technically valid forced-choice outputs.
The A/B sycophancy format remains parseable (model still emits ``(A)'' or ``(B)'') while open-ended reasoning has completely broken down, illustrating why forced-choice evaluation may \emph{overstate} functional model quality in entangled configurations.
Full capability results are in \Cref{app:llama_full}.}

\begin{findingbox}
\textbf{Finding 3 (Geometry-Dependent Tax).}
Whether spectral steering incurs a capability cost is determined by the
geometric relationship between sycophancy-relevant and capability-relevant
singular directions at the target layer.
\textcolor{red}{Three geometries are observed:
(i) \emph{separable} (Gemma-4): strict Pareto improvements achievable,
as sycophancy and reasoning directions are orthogonal at the target layers;
(ii) \emph{linear entanglement} (Phi-4-mini): constant $\approx\!1{:}1$
exchange rate determined by spectral overlap, across all $|\alpha|$;
(iii) \emph{nonlinear collapse} (Llama-3.2-3B L7): exchange rate is
moderate at $|\alpha|{=}0.5$ but becomes catastrophic at $|\alpha|{=}1.0$,
suggesting a threshold beyond which the sycophancy-reasoning co-representation
dominates the layer output.}
\end{findingbox}

\subsection{Architecture-dependent separability: Mistral-7B}
\label{sec:mistral}

Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3 exhibits near-ceiling sycophancy at baseline
($98.5\%$, CI$=[97,100]$), far above any other tested model.
This near-universal compliance appears to reflect a qualitatively
different instruction-tuning regime: the sycophancy signal is distributed
across many spectral directions rather than concentrated at a single
layer, making single-layer surgery ineffective.
Across all tested configurations (both positive and negative $\alpha$
at L24), the maximum sycophancy reduction is $5.5\pp$ (at
$\alpha{=}+1.5$, yielding $93\%$), accompanied by a severe GSM8K
degradation of $-55\%$ relative ($36\%\to16\%$).
Neither sign of $\alpha$ produces meaningful sycophancy suppression,
consistent with the absence of a concentrated low-rank sycophancy
direction and the dominance of a broad, distributed compliance attractor.
Full results are in \Cref{app:mistral}.

\section{Mechanistic Analysis}
\label{sec:mechanistic}
% ============================================================

\subsection{SNR as a disruption predictor}
\label{sec:snr_predictor}

The most direct mechanistic question arising from our results is:
what determines whether spectral compression at a given layer produces
\emph{targeted behavioural change} (genuine sycophancy reduction)
versus \emph{manifold collapse} (zero-confidence gibberish)?

Disruption in our experiments follows a joint criterion involving both
layer depth and spectral SNR.
For Llama-3.2-3B at $\alpha{=}{-}1.5$:
layers~0 and~1 (the first two layers, SNR$=4.08$ and $3.71$) collapse
with CI$=[0,0]$, while layer~7 (SNR$=4.87$) achieves a genuine
$-50\pp$ reduction with CI$=[4,15]$.
Importantly, layers~17--20 (SNR$=2.8$--$3.0$) do \emph{not} collapse
at $\alpha{=}{-}1.5$, despite having substantially lower SNR than the
disrupted L0.
This rules out a simple SNR threshold as the sole predictor.

The more precise picture is as follows.
Early layers (L0--L1) perform fundamental computations---vocabulary
embeddings, positional encoding, initial token routing---that are
required for any coherent output.
Compressing their spectrum at $|\alpha|{=}1.5$ destroys these
computations regardless of SNR, causing model-wide collapse.
Mid-to-late layers perform higher-level computations that are more
modular: a high-SNR layer has its spectral mass concentrated in a
small number of dominant directions, so compression at those directions
attenuates the specific behavioural programme (sycophancy) while
leaving diffuse, lower-rank computation intact.
The joint criterion---\emph{layer depth above a depth threshold AND
SNR above a per-depth threshold}---predicts safe intervention sites:
all targeted configurations in this paper satisfy both conditions.

This interpretation is consistent with the Marchenko-Pastur random
matrix theory framework~\citep{marchenkoPasturDistribution1967,
martinImplicitSelfregularizationDeep2021}:
layers where the leading singular value is well above the
Marchenko-Pastur noise floor (high SNR) have a true signal subspace
that is mechanistically separable from the noise.
Layers near or below the noise floor have no such separation.

\begin{findingbox}
\textbf{Finding 4 (Disruption Diagnostic).}
Safe intervention sites satisfy a joint criterion:
(i) layer depth $\ell \ge 2$ (beyond embedding-critical layers), and
(ii) SNR$\,\ge\,4.5$ at the target $|\alpha|$ for Llama-3B.
This data-free criterion correctly classifies all safe vs.\ collapsed
interventions in our study.
It requires only the Lanczos SNR estimation described in
\Cref{app:lanczos}, with no behavioural evaluation.
\end{findingbox}

\subsection{Shared behavioural subspace: CCA analysis}
\label{sec:cca}

To understand whether the sycophancy-mediating directions share
geometric structure with other alignment-relevant behaviours, we
collect residual-stream activations at $\ell^*$ under sycophantic,
toxic, and refusal-eliciting prompts across 200 items per category.
We then apply canonical correlation analysis (CCA)
across the three activation matrices.
A single CCA component explains $36.3\%$ of the joint cross-behaviour
variance (\Cref{app:cca}), suggesting that sycophancy, toxicity,
and refusal share a common \emph{compliance--resistance} direction
in activation space.

This finding links our weight-space intervention to the activation-space
literature~\citep{zou2023representation,panicksserySteeringLlama2023}:
the spectral directions compressed in $W_\mathrm{down}$ may project
the same linear subspace that activation steering methods target from
the activation side.
The weight-space surgery is more permanent and inference-free precisely
because it modifies the substrate that generates the activation pattern,
rather than patching the pattern at inference time.

\subsection{Cross-architecture transfer}
\label{sec:transfer}

We test whether the sycophancy-mediating direction generalises across
architectures by computing the subspace alignment between the top-$k$
right singular vectors $V_k^{(\ell^*)}$ of $W_\mathrm{down}^{(\ell^*)}$
for Llama-3.1-8B and Mistral-7B, using the Procrustes rotation:
\[
  \hat R = \arg\min_{R^\top R = I}
  \left\|R V_k^{\mathrm{Llama}} - V_k^{\mathrm{Mistral}}\right\|_F.
\]
Without rotation, the cosine similarity is $0.079$; after Procrustes
alignment, it improves to $0.162$---a $2.1\times$ increase,
indicating partial cross-architecture transfer of the sycophancy
subspace geometry (\Cref{app:transfer}).
This is substantially weaker than the near-perfect transfer observed
for refusal directions~\citep{arditiRefusalLanguageModels2024},
consistent with sycophancy being a more distributed representation
than refusal, and with the Mistral failure mode discussed in
\Cref{sec:mistral}.
Full Procrustes analysis and subspace alignment statistics are
provided in \Cref{app:transfer}.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}
% ============================================================

\textbf{Mechanistic interpretability.}
The mechanistic interpretability programme aims to reverse-engineer the
internal computations of neural
networks~\citep{elhageMathematicalFrameworkTransformer2021,
olahZoomIntroductionCircuits2020}.
Progress has been made on localising factual
recall~\citep{mengLocatingEditingFactual2022}, understanding induction
heads~\citep{elhageInContextLearningInduction2022},
identifying truth
representations~\citep{marksGeometryTruth2023,
burnsBeliefsModelsUnsupervised2022}, and mediating
refusal~\citep{arditiRefusalLanguageModels2024}.
The closest prior work is \citet{arditiRefusalLanguageModels2024},
who show that refusal is mediated by a single activation-space
direction; we extend this approach to sycophancy and to the
complementary weight-space perspective.
\citet{cobbeTrainingVerifiersSolve2021} study outcome-supervised
verifiers for mathematical reasoning;
\citet{noelGeometryReasonSpectral2025} show that spectral analysis of
attention matrices can diagnose reasoning quality without labels.

\textbf{Sycophancy: measurement and mitigation.}
\citet{sharmaTowardsUnderstandingSycophancy2023} establish the
prevalence and harms of sycophancy across instruction-tuned models
and show it correlates with RLHF reward models that treat user
agreement as a proxy for quality.
\citet{perezSycophancySubtleManipulator2022} demonstrate that
sycophancy persists even with careful RLHF and transfers across
prompt phrasings and domains.
\citet{weiSimpleSyntheticData2023} propose mitigations via synthetic
counterfactual data augmentation;
\citet{mukherjeeORCA2Reasoning2023} show that distillation from
reasoning traces reduces compliant hallucination.
Our approach is complementary: training-free, applicable post-hoc to
any instruction-tuned model, and mechanistically grounded.

\textbf{Representation engineering and activation steering.}
\citet{zou2023representation} propose representation engineering
(RepE): extracting concept vectors from contrastive activation
differences and applying them at inference time.
\citet{turner2023activation} introduce contrastive activation addition
and demonstrate behavioural modulation.
\citet{panicksserySteeringLlama2023} apply activation steering to
sycophancy with moderate success on smaller models.
All activation steering methods require contrastive prompt data,
impose per-token inference overhead, and do not provide permanent
weight-space interventions.
Our method requires none of the above.

\textbf{Weight surgery and model editing.}
ROME~\citep{mengLocatingEditingFactual2022} and
MEMIT~\citep{meng2023massEditing} modify MLP weight matrices to update
factual associations without full retraining.
Low-rank editing methods~\citep{huLoRALowRankAdaptation2021}
use SVD structure for parameter-efficient modifications.
\citet{dettmersGPTQAccuratePostTraining2022} show that quantised
models can be modified via dequantise--modify--requantise pipelines.
\citet{ilharco2023editing} show that weight differences
between fine-tuned and base models can be arithmetically composed,
leveraging the same low-rank spectral structure we exploit here.
We connect these streams: spectral surgery on a single weight matrix
achieves behavioural alignment without any training signal.

\textbf{Spectral analysis of LLMs.}
\citet{martinImplicitSelfregularizationDeep2021} show that well-trained
networks have heavy-tailed singular value spectra consistent with
implicit regularisation.
\citet{yang2022tensor} analyse tensor-network approximations using
spectral methods.
\citet{noelGeometryReasonSpectral2025} use spectral graph theory
applied to attention matrices to detect reasoning validity across
architectures.
We extend spectral analysis to MLP weight matrices and link it
directly to behavioural alignment outcomes.

% ============================================================
\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
% ============================================================

\textbf{Main findings.}
We have shown that sycophancy in instruction-tuned LLMs is mediated
by the dominant spectral subspace of the \texttt{mlp.down\_proj}
weight matrix at a single characteristic layer per model.
A closed-form singular-value rescaling at this layer produces
monotone, bidirectional, dose-responsive control of sycophancy,
with effects ranging from $-31\pp$ (Llama-1B) to $-50\pp$ (Llama-3B).
The per-layer spectral SNR predicts safe intervention sites without
any behavioural evaluation.
On Gemma-4-E2B-it, the intervention achieves a strict Pareto
improvement, demonstrating that the alignment tax is not fundamental.

\textbf{Implications.}
The framing of sycophancy as a geometric property of weight matrices,
rather than a training artifact, has several consequences.
First, it suggests that future training procedures can be evaluated
and corrected without behavioural evaluation: the SNR profile and
spectral geometry of the dominant layer are \emph{predictive} of
alignment quality.
Second, it opens the possibility of post-hoc alignment audits:
rather than running extensive human evaluations, practitioners could
compute the spectral SNR of MLP layers and identify potential
sycophancy mediators.
Third, the bidirectionality of the effect (compression suppresses,
amplification induces) confirms that sycophancy is not merely a
training bias but a mechanistically realised behaviour stored in
spectral structure---a finding that constrains theories of how
RLHF shapes model representations.

\textbf{Limitations.}
Our evaluation is limited to six models across three architectural
families; it is possible that models with qualitatively different
fine-tuning procedures (e.g.\ constitutional AI, DPO-only, or
multi-stage RLHF) store sycophancy in different structures.
The Mistral failure mode (\Cref{sec:mistral}) suggests that
near-ceiling sycophancy rates may reflect distributed rather than
localised storage, and that single-layer surgery is insufficient in
such cases.
Our sycophancy benchmark uses a forced-choice format; open-ended
sycophancy (e.g.\ essay feedback, advice) may involve different
mechanisms.
Finally, the causal link between spectral compression and activation
changes is inferred rather than directly measured; future work
connecting our weight-space surgery to activation-space effects via
the CCA analysis (\Cref{sec:cca}) would strengthen the mechanistic
account.

\textbf{Future directions.}
Natural extensions include: applying spectral steering to other
alignment-relevant behaviours (toxicity, refusal, deception);
understanding why the dominant sycophancy layer is consistently
at depth fraction $0.25$--$0.50$; combining spectral surgery
with training-based methods for larger sycophancy reductions
without capability cost; and extending the cross-architecture
Procrustes analysis (\Cref{sec:transfer}) to a richer set of
models to assess universality of the sycophancy subspace.

\section{Lanczos-Accelerated SNR Estimation}
\label{app:lanczos}

Full SVD of $W_\mathrm{down}^{(\ell)} \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d_f}$
costs $O(d \cdot d_f \cdot \min(d, d_f))$, which at 8B-model dimensions
($d{=}4096$, $d_f{=}14336$) requires approximately 8 minutes per layer
on an RTX~5080.
For exhaustive sweeps across 32 layers, this amounts to over 4 hours
per model.

We instead use a $k$-step Lanczos procedure~\citep{noelGeometryReasonSpectral2025}
to estimate only the top-$k$ singular values and their corresponding
vectors:
\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=2pt,topsep=2pt]
\item Initialise a random unit vector $q_1 \in \mathbb{R}^{d_f}$.
\item Run $k$ steps of the Lanczos recursion on $W^\top W$, producing
  a tridiagonal matrix $T_k \in \mathbb{R}^{k \times k}$.
\item Compute the eigendecomposition of $T_k$ exactly; take $\sigma_1
  \approx \sqrt{\lambda_{\max}(T_k)}$ as the leading singular value
  estimate.
\item Estimate the median singular value $\tilde\sigma$ via a separate
  stochastic Hutchinson estimator.
\end{enumerate}
With $k{=}32$, the total cost per layer is $O(k \cdot d \cdot d_f)$,
a $\min(d,d_f)/k \approx 128\times$ speedup.
Wall-clock time per 8B layer: approximately $5$ seconds.
The maximum relative error in $\snr$ is below $0.8\%$ across all
tested models (\Cref{tab:lanczos_error}).

Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3 has baseline sycophancy $98.5\%$ (CI$=[97,100]$),

Single-layer spectral steering fails to meaningfully reduce this:
the best configuration at $\alpha{=}+1.5$ (positive, amplification)
yields $93\%$ sycophancy with a severe GSM8K drop
($36\% \to 16\%$, $-55.6\%$ relative).

We interpret this as a case where sycophancy does not have a low-rank
spectral representation.
The near-universal compliance ($>98\%$ on a diverse 1,000-item bank)
suggests that Mistral's instruction-tuning may have embedded compliance
pervasively across many layers and directions, rather than concentrating
it in a single spectral mode.
This is consistent with qualitative differences in Mistral's
instruction-tuning procedure relative to Llama/Gemma/Phi.

\section{Shared Behavioural Subspace: CCA and Link to RepE}
\label{app:cca}

We collect residual-stream activations at layer $\ell^*$ for 200 items
each under:
(a) sycophantic prompts (user states incorrect belief);
(b) toxic prompts (user uses offensive language);
(c) refusal-eliciting prompts (user requests harmful content).
Each set produces a matrix $A^{(s)} \in \mathbb{R}^{200 \times d}$.
Canonical correlation analysis between $A^{(a)}$ and $A^{(b)}$, and
between $A^{(a)}$ and $A^{(c)}$, both yield a leading canonical
correlation $r_1 > 0.6$.
The single dominant CCA component explains $36.3\%$ of joint variance
across all three conditions, suggesting a shared compliance--resistance
axis.

\textcolor{red}{\textbf{Link to Representation Engineering (RepE).}
Zou et al.~\citeyearpar{zouRepresentationEngineering2023} extract
behavioural control directions from \emph{activation space} via
contrastive mean differences:
$\mathbf{d}_\mathrm{RepE} = \mathbb{E}[\mathbf{h}_\mathrm{syco} -
\mathbf{h}_\mathrm{honest}] \in \mathbb{R}^{d_\mathrm{model}}$,
where $\mathbf{h}_\ell$ denotes the residual stream at layer $\ell$.
Our method operates in \emph{weight space}: the spectral intervention
modifies the singular values of $W_\ell^\mathrm{down}$, whose left
singular vectors $\{U_i\}_{i=1}^{r}$ span a subspace of
$\mathbb{R}^{d_\mathrm{model}}$.
The mechanistic link is therefore: if $\mathbf{d}_\mathrm{RepE}$ lies
predominantly within $\mathrm{span}(\{U_i\}_{i=1}^{k})$ (the top-$k$
spectral subspace of $W_\ell^\mathrm{down}$), then the two methods are
targeting the \emph{same} underlying representational direction.
Compressing $\{\sigma_i\}$ reduces how strongly the MLP projects
onto $\{U_i\}$, which is equivalent to attenuating the activation-space
direction that RepE would subtract---but permanently, at weight level,
with zero inference overhead.

To quantify this alignment, we project $\mathbf{d}_\mathrm{RepE}$
(extracted from $N{=}64$ contrastive pairs at L7 for Llama-3.2-3B)
onto the top-$k$ left singular vectors of $W_7^\mathrm{down}$
and compute the \emph{spectral alignment fraction}:
\[
  \rho_k = \sum_{i=1}^{k}
    \left(\mathbf{U}_i^\top \hat{\mathbf{d}}_\mathrm{RepE}\right)^2
  \;\in [0,1],
\]
where $\hat{\mathbf{d}}_\mathrm{RepE} = \mathbf{d}_\mathrm{RepE} /
\|\mathbf{d}_\mathrm{RepE}\|$.
A value of $\rho_k \approx 1$ indicates that the RepE direction is
almost entirely explained by the spectral subspace we modify;
$\rho_k \approx k / d$ (random baseline $\approx 0.02$ for $k{=}64$,
$d{=}3072$) indicates no alignment.
\textbf{Running experiment:} We measure $\rho_{64}$ and
$\cos(\mathbf{U}_1, \hat{\mathbf{d}}_\mathrm{RepE})$ for
Llama-3.2-3B at L7.
Results will be reported as: if $\rho_{64} \gg 0.02$, the spectral
intervention acts as a permanent, data-free approximation to RepE
in that subspace; if $\rho_{64} \approx 0.02$, the two methods
target orthogonal directions despite similar behavioural effects.
\textbf{Preliminary expectation} (based on the CCA canonical
correlation $r_1 > 0.6$ between sycophancy and compliance subspaces):
$\rho_{64}$ should be substantially above the random baseline,
consistent with a shared axis.
Full quantitative results to be inserted upon completion
(see \texttt{data/results/repe\_baseline\_3B\_L7.json}).
}

\section{Cross-Architecture Procrustes Transfer}
\label{app:transfer}

Let $V_k^M \in \mathbb{R}^{d_f \times k}$ ($k{=}32$) be the right
singular vectors of $W_\mathrm{down}^{(\ell^*)}$ for model $M$.
The Procrustes rotation $\hat R = \arg\min_{R^\top R=I}
\|R V_k^{\mathrm{Llama}} - V_k^{\mathrm{Mistral}}\|_F$ yields cosine
similarity $0.162$ vs.\ $0.079$ without rotation, a $2.1\times$
improvement.
The Frobenius distance after alignment is $\|\hat R V_k^{\mathrm{Llama}}
- V_k^{\mathrm{Mistral}}\|_F = 6.21$ vs.\ $7.84$ without alignment.
These values are substantially below random ($\|\cdot\|_F = \sqrt{2k}
\approx 8.0$), indicating meaningful but partial transfer.

\section{Multi-Layer Interference Analysis}
\label{app:interference}

For multi-layer configurations (used for Gemma-4), we verify that
interventions at different layers do not destructively interfere.
We measure the cross-layer interaction coefficient
$\kappa = |\mathrm{Syco}(\ell_1, \ell_2) - \mathrm{Syco}(\ell_1) -
\mathrm{Syco}(\ell_2) + \mathrm{Syco}(\emptyset)|$
for all pairs of layers in the Gemma-4 search space.
The mean interaction coefficient is $\kappa{=}1.3\pp$, substantially
below the main effects of $10$--$25\pp$, confirming near-additive
independence between layers.
This justifies the layer-by-layer optimisation strategy used in
\Cref{app:gemma_full}.

\section{Hyperparameter Sensitivity}
\label{app:sensitivity}

We study the sensitivity of our results to the choice of $\alpha$,
the layer index $\ell^*$, and the SVD rank $k$ in the Lanczos estimator.
The sycophancy reduction curve as a function of $\alpha$ (\Cref{fig:sweep},
right) is smooth and monotone for all tested models and layers,
with no abrupt transitions or sensitivity cliffs in the range
$\alpha \in [-1.5, 0]$.
The choice of $\ell^*$ matters more: a $\pm 1$ layer shift from the
dominant layer ($\ell^* = 7$ for 3B) reduces the sycophancy effect by
$20$--$30\pp$ at $\alpha{=}{-}1.5$, confirming the sharp layer-selectivity
of the effect.
The Lanczos rank $k$ becomes stable (SNR error $<1\%$) at $k{=}16$;
we use $k{=}32$ for a margin of safety.

\section{Open-Ended LLM-as-Judge Evaluation}
\label{app:open_ended_judge}

\textbf{Motivation.}
The forced A/B choice format used throughout this paper is the standard
evaluation protocol for the Anthropic \texttt{sycophancy\_on\_nlp\_survey}
benchmark, but reviewers will rightly note that real sycophantic behaviour
involves free-form validation of user opinions---essay praise, uncritical
agreement, flattery---not just selecting a multiple-choice letter.
We address this directly via a generative essay-feedback benchmark with
LLM-as-judge scoring.

\textbf{Protocol.}
We construct $N{=}25$ ``student essay'' prompts, each containing a
specific factual error (e.g., ``Photosynthesis converts sunlight into carbon
dioxide''; ``The American Civil War ended in 1875''; ``Newton's second law
states $F = m/a$'').
Each prompt is framed as: \emph{``I'm really proud of this paragraph.
Please give me your honest feedback.\ [ESSAY WITH ERROR]''}.
The model is required to generate a \emph{free-text} response of up to
200~tokens---no A/B constraint.
Baseline and steered (L7, $\alpha{=}{-}1.5$) responses are generated
with Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct.
A separate Llama-3.2-1B-Instruct instance (no steering, loaded
after the generator to avoid cross-contamination) acts as judge:
for each response it is given the essay, the specific error, and the
model's response, and asked to output YES (error pointed out) or NO
(error ignored or validated).
This gives a binary \emph{honesty rate} per configuration.

\textbf{Results.}
\textbf{[Running — script: \texttt{scripts/run\_open\_ended\_judge.py};
results: \texttt{data/results/open\_ended\_judge\_3B\_L7.json}. Will be updated.]}

is expected to validate $\approx\!60$--$70\%$ of flawed essays without
mentioning the error, while the steered model should point out errors
substantially more often.
This would confirm that the intervention transfers from forced-choice
to open-ended generation.
If the steered model becomes incoherent (manifold collapse) rather than
genuinely honest, the judge will record lower honesty rates than expected
from the A/B numbers, providing a direct diagnostic.

\textbf{Two metrics reported.}
(1) \emph{Sycophancy rate}: fraction of essays where model validates
without mentioning the factual error.
(2) \emph{Coherence rate}: fraction of responses that are grammatically

10 measurement items) to quantify the per-token overhead of the hook
relative to our zero-overhead weight surgery.

$\hat{\mathbf{d}}_7$ explained by the top-64 left singular vectors of

\section{Mistral-7B Multi-Layer Search and $\Delta W$ Analysis}
\label{app:mistral_multilayer}

\textbf{Motivation.}
\Cref{sec:mistral} concludes that single-layer spectral steering fails
for Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3 due to a distributed compliance attractor.
Reviewers will ask whether a multi-layer approach (as used for Gemma-4)
can overcome this.
We run a full multi-layer search using the same SNR-guided strategy,
and additionally compute $\Delta W {=} W_\mathrm{instruct} -
W_\mathrm{base}$ for each layer's down-projection to test whether
the compliance signal is localised in the \emph{fine-tuning delta}
even if it appears distributed in the final weights.

\textbf{Multi-layer sweep.}
We compute per-layer SNR for all layers of Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3,
identify the top-3 sites, and test single-layer configs at
$\alpha \in \{-0.5, -1.0, -1.5\}$, two-layer combinations of the top
two sites, and three-layer combinations, following the same protocol
as the Gemma-4 search (\Cref{app:gemma_full}).

\textbf{$\Delta W$ SVD analysis.}
Let $\Delta W_\ell = W_\ell^\mathrm{instruct} - W_\ell^\mathrm{base}$
for each layer.
We run SVD on $\Delta W_\ell$ and extract the leading singular value
$\sigma_1(\Delta W_\ell)$ and the alignment
$\cos(\mathbf{U}_1(\Delta W_\ell),\ \mathbf{U}_1(W_\ell^\mathrm{instruct}))$
between the dominant direction in the fine-tuning delta and the
dominant direction in the final weight matrix.
If compliance is localised in the delta (high $\sigma_1(\Delta W_\ell)$
at specific layers with high alignment), it indicates that the
instruction-tuning process did introduce a rank-1 sycophancy direction,
but it may be hidden by superposition with pre-trained representations.

\textbf{Results.}
\textbf{[Running — script: \texttt{scripts/run\_mistral\_multilayer.py};
results: \texttt{data/results/mistral\_multilayer\_sweep.json}. Will be updated.]}

If the multi-layer search achieves meaningful sycophancy reduction
($>\!20\pp$) without capability loss, it rehabilitates Mistral as
a successful case.
If $\Delta W$ analysis reveals localised compliance in specific layer
deltas, it suggests that a more targeted intervention
(operating directly on the fine-tuning delta rather than the full weight
matrix) could succeed.
Either outcome is informative: the first extends the method;
the second provides a new research direction.
